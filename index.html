How SPORE works

1

Content Enters Dormancy

A creator uploads their work — music, art, film, writing. It becomes a Dormant File: a blurred preview, a 30-second clip, a pixelated image. The full experience is sealed inside.

2

Proximity Handshake

To share a Dormant File, two people must be physically co-located. Devices confirm presence via NFC tap or BLE spatial awareness — then both SPORES simultaneously place a fingerprint on each other's device screen, providing a dual-biometric confirmation that two real humans are there.

3

The File Blooms

Content unlocks only after both SPORES experience it together in its entirety — the full track plays, the full article is read aloud, the full video runs — with devices in proximity and biometrics active throughout. Only then is the full file permanently unlocked for solo access, and the connection written to the Mycelium Network on-chain.

4

Organic Spread

That person can now share with their in-person circle. Each hop is verified, each connection traceable back to the Source. Content travels like spores — organically, authentically, through real human networks.

Technology

The infrastructure of
real connection

⛓️

EVM-Compatible Chain

All connections, content transfers, and bond states are written to an EVM-compatible blockchain — immutable, transparent, and verifiable by anyone.

🪙

SPORE Coin (ERC-20)

The native token powering the ecosystem. Earned through real-world engagement, content unlocks, and contribution to the network — not speculation.

🎴

Content NFTs (ERC-721)

Every piece of content is minted as an NFT with its full propagation history encoded. Provenance is permanent and human-verified from Source to recipient.

🗂️

IPFS + Filecoin

Media stored on IPFS with Filecoin backing. Censorship-resistant, decentralised, and retrievable as long as the network exists — your content isn't owned by a server.

📋

Genesis Hash Registry

Every content source is registered with a genesis hash at upload — an immutable fingerprint proving the original creator and timestamp of any piece of media on the network.

🏅

SporeBadgeAuthority

On-chain smart contract encoding the logic that distinguishes a Source Spore (original creator content) from an Echo Spore (propagated content). Objective, publicly readable status — cryptographic proof of origin, not a platform label.
